Learning when younger vs. older

Since juggling is just practicing motor skills, it is much easier for a developing mind to learn how to juggle. As we get older, the human brain tends to make connections to previous experiences to try find an easier path and juggling is probably a completely new experience to some adults. That is why as we get older it might be harder to learn new skills that are completely out of our comfort zone. For example there are 2 people who both did not grow up with juggling, person A who grew up practicing gymnastics and person B who did not. Person A would most likely have a easier time learning how to juggle compared to person B because person A had better hand eye coordination from when they were little.

Understanding the Exercises

In each tab up above there is a collection of techniques that are heavily used when learning how to juggle. The tab for the one ball, two balls and three balls are mostly just learning exercises while the special tab is more for the professionals. For beginners it is recommended to start at the beginning, which is the first tab next to this one, it is also recommended that the ball that is being thrown into the air should be at eye level unless specified otherwise. Each exercise is listed in order from easiest to hardest and it is best to save your progress of what you have accomplished in the drop down box and scrolling down and clicking "save". Some exercises may seem tedious and boring but it is very important to keep doing them. You are not just training your mind, but the nerves that connect the two and response from one to another.

Fine Motor Skills

Juggling can be used to increase motor function with almost everything in everyday life. For example, lets say you want to show off a little in front of some friends well you would be able to grab your water bottle and toss it behind your back, over your shoulder and catch it with your other hand and act like it is nothing happened. Or lets say there is something being thrown in your direction, you are usually left with one option which is to dodge it. Now that you know how to juggle, your hand eye coordination is good enough to catch it, and maybe show off a little.
