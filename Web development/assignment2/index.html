The Four-Man Trio is a group of 5 men that attended Southland High School is rural Adams Minnesota and were in the graduating class of 2015. In 2012, the Four-Man Trio was just that, a four man singing group consisting of Marvin Heimer, Colten Allen, Boyd Mortenson, and Maxwell Tapp. In 2013, Joseph Goergen joined high school choir and ended up joining the group and all decided to stick with the original name since it seemed funny and unorthodox.

The group of 5 men very much enjoyed singing as a group, at first to get attention but then they quickly built a fan-base from there. The men started out singing as a group with the help of Jenna Steinkamp who would either compose the men, play the songs on the piano for them or both at the same time. The Four-Man Trio also enjoyed not only just singing along with a piano but without one as well, they loved singing acapella not only for the sound that is produced but also for the difficulty that brought along with singing without instruments.

The group of 5 would go to contests to compete with other groups and usually came back victorious with a Superior or the highest performance rank. At these competitions, the men would like to have fun, make friends with other choir people from other schools and could be seen doing ridiculous things such as going up and down elevators singing for each floor the door opens up to. Sometimes Marvin Heimer would bring his guitar and get not just the Trio, but around 15 random people to sing songs that most people know.

In 2015, the men's senior year of high school, they decided to go all out and with the help of Kari Kuehneman, they decided to make album covers and T-Shirts. The men themselves have exclusive t-shirts and even some album covers that have not yet been released...until today.
